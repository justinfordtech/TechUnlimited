Phishing Scam Targeting Facebook Users

by justin fordSept 11th, 2021

There are fraudulent messages being spread through Facebook, Email and even through text messaging on cell phones. This type of fraud is known as a Phishing Attack. The scammer sends a seemingly innocent message, such as the one pictured below, with a link to click. The link will direct the victim to a fake login page which looks almost identical to Facebook’s real login page. The victim will enter their sign-in credentials which gives them straight to the scammer. The scammer will then hijack the victim’s account. This is how 90% of Facebook accounts get “hacked”.

Below are two images. the first is a fake page and the second is a genuine page – can you spot the differences?

Here are some differences I found. Did you spot more?
1. The fake page calls itself “Log in” while there is no space in “Login” on the real page.
2. The URL, obviously, is not Facebook's.
3. There are more language options on the real page.
4. Forgot spelled “Forgotten” on fake page.
